1. Diagnostic Equipment & Sensors

Thermal Camera

Captures surface temperature and cooling rate of the weld bead and Heat-Affected Zone (HAZ).

Clamp Meter

Measures electrical current (A) and voltage (V) without disconnecting the welding circuit.

LVDT Sensor

Linear Variable Differential Transformer used to measure structural displacement and distortion.

Arc Welding Analyzer

Records high-speed load and force dynamics generated during the welding process.

3. Electrical Scatter Diagrams & Analysis

Plot A: Current vs Voltage

Analysis: Positive correlation. As the current increases, the welding machine automatically increases the voltage to maintain a consistent arc length.

Plot B: Current vs Power

Analysis: Strong positive linear correlation. Total welding power surges significantly as both increase.

Plot C: Current vs Heat Input

Analysis: Positive correlation. Sufficient heat produces deep penetration. However, excessive heat widens the Heat-Affected Zone (HAZ), makes the metal brittle, and causes distortion.

4. Statistical Process Control (Thermal Analysis)

X-Bar Control Chart for Temperature Stability

Control Chart Interpretation:
  • The vast majority of the sample means (23 points) deviate beyond the acceptable statistical thermal boundaries.
  • Although the R-chart shows intra-sample stability, the X-bar chart reveals severe inter-sample instability.
  • The overall heat input across the production run is highly erratic and unpredictable due to erratic wire feed speed causing arc current fluctuations.
  • Corrective Action: Inspect and calibrate the wire feeder drive rolls and replace the worn contact tip.
Range (R) Max-Min Temperature Reading:

  • 0 points are above the UCL R.
  • Although the X-bar chart reveals severe inter-sample instability, the R-chart shows intra-sample stability (the sensor is reading consistently within each sample grouping).

Maximum Load

145 N

The maximum load recorded was 145 N, occurring precisely at 15 seconds. This peak load is the direct result of intense localized thermal expansion, which generates high internal stress within the metal during the welding process.

Maximum Displacement

1.02 mm

The maximum displacement recorded during welding was 1.02 mm and it occurred at Arc Stop. As heat accumulated during welding, the material expanded continuously, causing displacement to increase. The maximum displacement occurred immediately before the cooling process started.
